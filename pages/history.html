The Journey

A History of Greatness

From a debut in 1975 to World Champions in 1996 — and beyond.

Timeline

Key Moments in Sri Lanka Cricket

The milestones that shaped a nation's cricketing identity.

🏏
1975

First World Cup Appearance

Sri Lanka made their World Cup debut in the inaugural 1975 tournament in England, competing on the world stage for the first time.

🌟
1981

Full ICC Test Status

Sri Lanka became the eighth Test-playing nation, playing their first Test match against England at P. Saravanamuttu Stadium in Colombo.

💥
1994

Jayasuriya Revolution Begins

Sanath Jayasuriya began transforming ODI opening batting with explosive early hitting, laying the groundwork for a new era of aggressive cricket.

🏆
1996

World Cup Champions! 🎉

The defining moment. Sri Lanka won the Cricket World Cup in Lahore defeating Australia by 7 wickets. Aravinda de Silva scored an unbeaten century. Arjuna Ranatunga lifted the trophy. The nation celebrated for weeks.

🌀
2004

Murali Takes 500 Test Wickets

Muttiah Muralitharan became the first bowler to take 500 Test wickets — a milestone that signaled he was on his way to an unbreakable record.

📊
2007

World Cup Final — Second Time

Sri Lanka reached the World Cup final in the West Indies, losing narrowly to Australia in a rain-affected match, but demonstrated their consistent world-class quality.

🔢
2010

Murali's 800th Test Wicket

In his final Test match, Muttiah Muralitharan took his 800th Test wicket — the last ball of his career, a fairytale ending for the greatest spinner of all time.

🏆
2014

ICC T20 World Cup Champions!

Sri Lanka won the ICC World Twenty20 in Bangladesh, defeating India in the final. Lasith Malinga and Kumar Sangakkara led a masterful performance.

👋
2015

Sangakkara & Jayawardena Retire

The golden era ended as Kumar Sangakkara and Mahela Jayawardena retired, leaving behind extraordinary legacies and a nation in gratitude.

🌱
2022–Present

New Era with Hasaranga & Theekshana

A new generation of spinners — Wanindu Hasaranga and Maheesh Theekshana — are bringing Sri Lanka back to the top of world T20 rankings and carrying the flag forward.

Greatest Moment

The 1996 World Cup Story

How a small island shocked the entire cricket world.

✊

The Boycott Problem

Australia and West Indies refused to play in Sri Lanka due to security concerns, handing Sri Lanka two walkovers — but the team turned this adversity into motivation.

💣

The Pinch-Hitting Strategy

Jayasuriya and Kaluwitharana opened batting with aggressive intent, scoring rapidly in the first 15 overs — a tactic that had never been seen before in world cricket.

🥊

The Semi-Final Drama

Against India in Calcutta (now Kolkata), bottles were thrown onto the pitch and the match was awarded to Sri Lanka — one of the most controversial moments in World Cup history.

🏆

The Final Victory

Against Australia in Lahore, Aravinda de Silva scored 107 not out. Sri Lanka won by 7 wickets with 22 balls to spare. Arjuna Ranatunga walked off the field a world champion.
